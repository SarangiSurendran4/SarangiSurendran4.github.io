Philosophy

How I approach documentation

Good documentation isn't about writing — it's about understanding where users get stuck and removing that friction. I treat every article as a product artifact with its own adoption curve.

That means starting from support data, not assumptions. Shipping alongside the feature, not after. Measuring engagement, not just word count.

01

Identify friction

Analyze L1 tickets, CallAI transcripts, and usage analytics to find where users are stuck before I write a word.

02

Validate with users

Attend customer calls and usability sessions with DACs to confirm assumptions about how users actually think.

03

Create targeted content

Build docs and DAP guidance around real workflows — not feature component lists. Task-based, cross-linked, scenario-driven.

04

Measure and iterate

Use Whatfix analytics to track article engagement and feature adoption, then close the loop with content updates.

Release Docs · Early Access Testing
Diagnostics: Restructuring docs around real debugging workflows
PM appreciation · Strong adoption
›

Diagnostics was a complex debugging feature. Technically correct documentation would be practically useless if it didn't map to what users actually experience when something breaks.

Onboarded as an early-access tester, reproduced real user scenarios, and listened to customer calls to understand how users think when debugging. Restructured the guide around that mental model — not the feature's component list.

Added a dedicated troubleshooting section cross-linked from every related error state. The PM noted the docs reflected the customer's thought process. The article became a team reference and the feature saw strong post-launch adoption.

Adoption: Turning documentation into a measurable engagement driver
100+ responses · ~15–20% adoption lift

A new feature had low adoption despite documentation existing. Users could read the docs and still not know what to do with the feature in their context.

Led usability testing with Digital Adoption Consultants — they confirmed articles were too abstract. Rewrote community posts around real scenarios, then surfaced them via a Whatfix Launcher on the support site, giving users a direct path from documentation to action.

Community traffic increased after launch. Feature feedback initiatives received 100+ responses. Usage signals improved measurably — approximately 15–20% lift in feature adoption.

Python · Selenium · Jira API · GAS

Documentation Health Automation Suite

Manual content audits were consuming significant team time every week — checking for broken links, aging articles, duplicate files, and tracking support ticket status. These were recurring, low-insight tasks that prevented the team from focusing on strategic documentation work.

What I built
  • Broken-link & broken-image crawler (Selenium + Python) — crawled all support articles across EN and DE projects, flagged broken links and GIFs, enabling faster fixes and early detection
  • Article-age tracker with auto-assignment — identified stale articles and automatically assigned update tasks via Jira API, removing the need for manual triage
  • WSC ticket reporter — automated fetching of Jira metrics and support ticket reports on a schedule, eliminating a recurring manual pull
  • Release-reminder emailer — automated reminders ahead of every release cycle so no release documentation was delayed
  • Duplicate-file highlighter — surface duplicate or near-duplicate content across the knowledge base
Outcome

Eliminated all recurring weekly manual audits across the team. Freed documentation bandwidth for higher-value strategic work. Scripts adopted team-wide and integrated into regular content operations.

Gen AI · Gemini · Zendesk · CallAI

AI-Powered Support Intelligence Workflow

Support ticket analysis was manual and time-consuming. Identifying recurring pain points, root causes, and documentation gaps across a high-volume ticket queue required reading through individual tickets one by one — making pattern detection slow and inconsistent.

What I built
  • Used Gen AI to analyze L1 support tickets at scale — identifying root causes, clustering recurring issues by feature area, and surfacing documentation gaps that weren't visible through manual review
  • Built reusable Gemini GEMs and prompt libraries for peer reviewing articles, drafting documentation, writing community posts, and analyzing CallAI call transcripts — making AI usage consistent and scalable across the team
  • Used AI to summarize long CallAI call transcripts, extracting key customer pain points and product insights without manual review of full recordings
  • Translated insights into targeted documentation and DAP interventions — closing gaps identified through the AI analysis pipeline
Outcome

Raised 6–7 product insights per quarter based on AI-assisted analysis — consistently exceeding targets. Reusable prompts enabled team-wide AI adoption. Reduced time spent on ticket triage and transcript review, with outputs validated for accuracy before use. 100% compliance with company AI usage guidelines across all implementations.

Usability Testing · Whatfix DAP · Community

Driving Feature Adoption Through Research-Led DAP

A newly released feature had low adoption. Standard documentation existed, but there was a gap between what the help articles covered and how customers actually used the feature in real-world scenarios. The community posts weren't surfacing at the right moment in the user journey.

What I did
  • Conducted a structured usability testing session with a Digital Adoption Consultant — uncovered specific confusion points, real use case patterns, and where users abandoned the feature workflow
  • Researched how other DAP company websites engage their communities and drive traffic, benchmarking engagement strategies across the industry
  • Built a Whatfix Launcher on the support site that surfaced contextually relevant community use cases at the moment users needed them — bridging the gap between help articles and real-world examples
  • Created 6 community posts grounded in real customer scenarios from the usability session, replacing generic feature walkthroughs with problem-first use case narratives
  • Shared a detailed insight report with the team and PM highlighting what changed, why, and what to iterate on next
Outcome

Community traffic and feature engagement increased after the Launcher was deployed. The usability session surfaced actionable gaps that weren't visible through ticket data alone. Established a repeatable research-to-DAP workflow for future feature releases.

UX Writing · Cross-pod · Governance

Product-Wide UX Writing Consistency Initiative

UX copy inconsistencies had accumulated across 7 product pods — different terminology for the same features, inconsistent button labels, conflicting microcopy patterns. This created confusion for users navigating between features and made documentation harder to maintain.

What I did
  • Proactively audited UI copy across all 7 pods — identifying terminology conflicts, inconsistent labels, and microcopy patterns that differed from documentation language
  • Built a consolidated tracking sheet cataloguing every inconsistency by pod, location, current state, and recommended fix — making the scope visible for the first time
  • Collaborated with the UX writer to co-develop updated UX writing guidelines that resolved the conflicts and established shared standards going forward
  • Proposed a product-level Smart Tip to surface guidance at a key Studio friction point — recognized on Slack for thinking beyond documentation into product experience
Outcome

Updated UX writing guidelines adopted across the product, improving terminology consistency for users and reducing documentation maintenance caused by copy drift. Recognized on Slack for identifying a product-level improvement opportunity rather than just documenting existing behavior.
